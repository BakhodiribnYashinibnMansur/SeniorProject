# Present simple — A1

> The first tense. By the end of this page you can introduce yourself, talk about your job, your family, your routine, and basic facts about the world.

[siblings: A2](A2.md) · [B1](B1.md) · [B2](B2.md) · [C1](C1.md) · [C2](C2.md) · [tricky](tricky.md) · [find errors](find-errors.md)

## 1 · The big idea

The present simple is for things that **stay true** — not for things happening *right now*.

> *I live in Tashkent.* — true today, will be true tomorrow.
> *She works in a hospital.* — that's her job, every day.
> *Water boils at 100 °C.* — fact about the world.

It is **not** the tense for *I am working right now*. That is the **present continuous**, which you'll meet next.

---

## 2 · Affirmative — making positive sentences

Take the verb in its base form. Add **`-s`** if the subject is `he`, `she`, or `it`.

| Subject | Verb | Example |
|---|---|---|
| I | work | *I **work** in a school.* |
| You | work | *You **work** hard.* |
| **He / She / It** | **work·s** | *She **works** in a school.* |
| We | work | *We **work** together.* |
| They | work | *They **work** late.* |

That `-s` for `he / she / it` is **the** thing to remember at this level.

### Two special verbs

| | I / You / We / They | He / She / It |
|---|---|---|
| **be** | I am · you / we / they are | she / he / it **is** |
| **have** | I / you / we / they have | she / he / it **has** |

> *I **am** a student.*
> *She **is** my sister.*
> *I **have** two brothers.*
> *She **has** a dog.*

## 3 · Negative — making "no" sentences

Use **`don't`** or **`doesn't`** plus the **base verb**.

| Subject | Negative |
|---|---|
| I / You / We / They | **don't** + base verb |
| He / She / It | **doesn't** + base verb |

> *I **don't like** coffee.*
> *They **don't live** here.*
> *He **doesn't speak** Russian.*
> *She **doesn't have** a phone.*

**Important rule:** the main verb stays in its base form. The `-s` moves to `does` and **disappears** from the verb. So we say *She **doesn't like** coffee* — never *She doesn't likes coffee*.

`be` is different — no `do/does`, just `not`:

> *I **am not** late.* (or *I'm not late.*)
> *She **isn't** here.*
> *They **aren't** ready.*

## 4 · Yes/no questions

Put **`Do`** or **`Does`** before the subject.

| | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| I / you / we / they | **Do** + subject + verb? | *Do you speak English?* |
| He / she / it | **Does** + subject + verb? | *Does she like tea?* |

> ***Do** you live here?* — *Yes, I do. / No, I don't.*
> ***Does** he work in a bank?* — *Yes, he does. / No, he doesn't.*

Same trick: the main verb stays in its base form. *Does she **like** tea?* — never *Does she likes tea?*

For `be`, no helper — just invert:

> ***Are** you tired?* — *Yes, I am.*
> ***Is** she a doctor?* — *Yes, she is.*

## 5 · What we use the present simple for at A1

### 5.1 · Habits and routines

Things you do regularly.

> *I **drink** coffee every morning.*
> *He **takes** the bus to work.*
> *We **don't watch** TV at night.*
> *She **reads** before bed.*

### 5.2 · General facts

Things that are always true.

> *Cats **drink** milk.*
> *The sun **rises** in the east.*
> *Bananas **are** yellow.*
> *Ice **doesn't melt** in the freezer.*

### 5.3 · Permanent personal information

Your name, your job, your home, your family, your likes.

> *My name **is** Bakhodir.*
> *I **come** from Uzbekistan.*
> *My father **works** as an engineer.*
> *I **like** football.*

## 6 · Frequency words — `always`, `sometimes`, `never`

Tell the listener **how often** something happens.

| Word | How often |
|---|---|
| **always** | every time, 100% |
| **sometimes** | not always, but often enough |
| **never** | not at any time, 0% |

**Position:** before the main verb — *I **always** read in the evening*. After `be` — *He **is always** late*.

> *I **always** brush my teeth before bed.*
> *We **sometimes** eat at restaurants.*
> *She **never** drinks alcohol.*
> *They **are always** kind.*

Don't say *I don't never read* — `never` is already negative. Just say *I **never** read*.

---

## 7 · Time expressions

These usually go at the **end** of the sentence:

- *every day · every Monday · every weekend*
- *in the morning · in the evening · at night*
- *on Mondays · at weekends*

> *I go to school **every day**.*
> *We have lunch **at one o'clock**.*
> *She studies **in the evening**.*

## 8 · Common A1 mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---|---|
| *He live in London.* | He **lives** in London. |
| *She have two brothers.* | She **has** two brothers. |
| *They works hard.* | They **work** hard. |
| *I am work in a bank.* | I **work** in a bank. |
| *She doesn't likes coffee.* | She **doesn't like** coffee. |
| *Do she like coffee?* | **Does** she like coffee? |
| *Does she likes coffee?* | Does she **like** coffee? |
| *She no like fish.* | She **doesn't like** fish. |
| *Where you live?* | Where **do** you live? |
| *I am have a brother.* | I **have** a brother. |

## 9 · Practice — try saying these out loud

1. Say two true sentences about your daily routine.
2. Say two true sentences about your family.
3. Say one fact about your country.
4. Make one negative sentence about something you don't do.
5. Ask a friend three questions: where they live, where they work, what they like.

When you can do all five comfortably, you have A1 present simple. Move to [A2](A2.md).
